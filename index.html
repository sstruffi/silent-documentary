Videography / Silent Portrait

Huether Hotel Portrait

A short silent documentary capturing everyday life at the Huether Hotel in Uptown Waterloo, including the café, lounge, and Jazz Room, and showing how different routines and environments come together in one place.

Silent Documentary Observational Filmmaking Visual Storytelling Editing

Film

The film focuses on the Huether Hotel as a place people move through in different ways throughout the day. In the morning, it captures quieter moments in the café, with coffee, pastries, and early guests. As the day progresses, the space becomes busier, with people meeting friends, working on laptops, passing through, and spending time in the Jazz Room.

Rather than using spoken explanation, the project tells the story visually through details like coffee being poured, cups placed on tables, people moving through the building, conversations, and live jazz instruments on display. The goal is to show the hotel as a place where different routines and environments come together.

Shooting Style

The shooting style is calm and observational, using mostly tripod shots. Wide shots show the space and how people move through the café, lounge, and bar. Medium shots focus on activity at the seating areas, café counter, and tables, while close-ups capture details like coffee being poured, hands typing, cups on tables, and jazz instruments.

A few slow-moving shots follow customers as they walk to the counter or bring drinks to their seats, but the camera stays subtle and out of the way. Instead of focusing on faces, many shots frame people from behind, over the shoulder, or through hands and movement to create emotion and tell the story.

Production Plan

The project was planned for the Huether Hotel and The Jazz Room at 59 King Street North in Uptown Waterloo. The production plan included evening filming in the Jazz Room for setup, performance, and relaxed guests, then morning and midday filming to capture the exterior, café preparation, empty or quieter interiors, and the busier lunch period with tables filling, guests passing through, and people eating and talking.

The planned equipment included a Canon R7, 18–150 mm lens, Canon SX730 HS, and a Manfrotto tripod. Personnel included friends acting naturally in the space, customers, and staff if possible.

Outcome

This project shows my interest in observational film and visual storytelling through mood, pacing, and detail. It focuses on building atmosphere through framing, movement, and editing rather than dialogue, and highlights the type of film work I want to keep exploring in my videography practice.
